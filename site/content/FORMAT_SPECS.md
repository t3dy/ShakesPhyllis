# Content Format Specifications

## 1. SONNET ANALYSES

**Purpose**: In-depth close reading of each sonnet as a literary object.

### Sections

1. **Sonnet Header** — Number, first line, FYM/DL indicator, group tag
2. **Full Text** — Complete sonnet with line numbers, quatrains/couplet divided
3. **Overview** (1-2 paragraphs) — What this sonnet is about, its place in the sequence, its argument
4. **Close Reading** — Quatrain-by-quatrain analysis:
   - Quatrain 1: What does it establish?
   - Quatrain 2: How does it develop or complicate?
   - Quatrain 3: Where does the argument turn?
   - Couplet: How does it resolve (or fail to)?
5. **Key Images & Language** — Specific words, metaphors, puns analyzed with verbatim citations
6. **FYM/DL Dynamics** — How this sonnet relates to the Fair Young Man or Dark Lady, what it reveals about the relationship
7. **Scholarly Perspectives** — Summary of 2-4 scholarly interpretations (Vendler, Booth, Kerrigan, Pequigney, etc.) with citations
8. **Connections** — Links to related sonnets, thematic echoes, sequence position

### Citation Style
- Verbatim sonnet lines in italics: *"Shall I compare thee to a summer's day?"* (line 1)
- Scholarly citations: Author, *Title* (Year), p. X — "quoted passage"
- Cross-references: See Sonnet 20 (Analysis)

## 2. SONNET DIRECTING

**Purpose**: Line-by-line performance guidance for directors and actors.

### Structure

For each of the 14 lines:
1. **Line text** (verbatim, in sonnet font)
2. **Meter** — Scan the line. Note inversions, feminine endings, caesurae, spondees. Mark stressed syllables.
3. **Emphasis** — Which words to stress for meaning. Where the rhetorical weight falls.
4. **Emotional Beat** — What the speaker is feeling/doing at this moment (persuading, pleading, mocking, despairing, etc.)
5. **Staging Note** — Physical/spatial suggestion (turn toward audience, pause, accelerate, intimate whisper, etc.)
6. **Subtext** — What's unsaid. The gap between words and meaning.

### Structural Notes (per sonnet)
- **Overall Arc**: The emotional journey from line 1 to line 14
- **The Volta**: Where it turns — how to physicalize the shift
- **The Couplet**: How to land the ending — punch, whisper, irony, tenderness?
- **Tempo Map**: Which sections move fast, which slow down

### Voice
Direct address to a director/actor. Second person. Imperative. "Stress 'summer's.' Pause after 'day.' Let the question hang."

## 3. SONNET CONTEXTS

**Purpose**: Everything outside the poem that illuminates it — historical, biographical, literary.

### Sections

1. **Date & Publication** — When likely composed, 1609 Quarto position, any manuscript evidence
2. **Biographical Context** — What was happening in Shakespeare's life (if knowable) around the time of composition
3. **The Fair Young Man Question** (for sonnets 1-126) — Which candidate(s) this sonnet supports, with evidence:
   - Southampton: Does the language fit his situation? Age? Patronage relationship?
   - Pembroke: Does the timeline work? Social dynamics?
   - Others: Willie Hughes, William Hart, etc.
4. **The Dark Lady Question** (for sonnets 127-152) — Same treatment:
   - Emilia Lanier: Musical references? Italian connections? Forman evidence?
   - Mary Fitton: Court connections? Physical description match?
   - Lucy Negro: Social milieu evidence?
   - Others
5. **Literary Context** — Sonnet tradition (Petrarch, Sidney, Spenser, Daniel), what Shakespeare is doing differently, intertextual echoes
6. **Cultural Moment** — Elizabethan/Jacobean social context relevant to the sonnet's themes (patronage, sexuality, plague, theater closure, etc.)
7. **Critical History** — How interpretation of this sonnet has changed over time (18th century bowdlerization, 20th century queer readings, etc.)

## 4. ESSAYS

**Purpose**: Standalone thematic essays on cross-cutting topics.

### Format
- Title
- Subtitle / one-line thesis
- SHWEP-style editorial voice: scholar-at-a-pub
- 2000-5000 words
- H2/H3 section structure
- Verbatim citations from the sonnets
- Scholarly citations with bibliography
- Cross-references to specific sonnet Analyses, Directing notes, or Contexts

1. The Fair Young Man: Identity, Evidence, and Why It Matters
2. The Dark Lady: Race, Desire, and the Limits of Identification
3. The Speaker's Sexuality: From Bowdlerization to Queer Reading

8. Procreation Sonnets: Persuasion, Patronage, and the Marriage Plot
